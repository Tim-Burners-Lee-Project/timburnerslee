Tim Berners-Lee

Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist who was born in London in 1955. He studied physics at Oxford University, where he developed an interest in computers and how they could be used to organise and share information more effectively.

The Problem

While working at CERN, a scientific research organisation, he noticed that scientists around the world were struggling to share information. Different computers stored data in different formats, making it difficult to access and connect ideas.

The Invention

In 1989, he proposed a system that would allow documents to be linked together and accessed through a network. This system became known as the World Wide Web. It allowed users to click links and move between pages, making information much easier to explore.

How It Worked

To make the web possible, he developed several key technologies. HTML was used to create the structure of web pages, URLs were used to give each page a unique address, and HTTP allowed computers to communicate and transfer data across the internet.

Impact

The invention of the web completely changed how people communicate and access information. It made it possible for anyone to publish content online and for users to access knowledge from anywhere in the world almost instantly.

A Crucial Decision

One of the most important decisions he made was not to patent the web. By making it free and open for everyone, it was able to grow rapidly and become the global system we rely on today.
