Tim Berners-Lee

A British computer scientist born in 1955 in London.

Early Life

He studied physics at Oxford University.

He was interested in computers and how they share information.

The Problem

Scientists around the world struggled to share data easily.

Different computers could not communicate well.

The Solution

In 1989, he proposed a system to link documents together.

This idea became the World Wide Web.

Key Technologies

HTML – structure of web pages

URL – addresses for pages

HTTP – communication between computers

First Website

The first website was created in 1991.

It explained how the web worked.

Impact

The web changed communication worldwide.

It allowed instant access to information.

It helped create the modern internet.

Important Choice

He made the web free for everyone.

This allowed it to grow rapidly across the world.
