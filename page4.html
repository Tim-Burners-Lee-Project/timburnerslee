What Is He Doing Now?

After inventing the World Wide Web, Tim Berners-Lee did not stop working on technology. Instead, he has continued to focus on improving the web and ensuring that it remains useful, fair, and accessible for everyone around the world.

An Open Web

One of his main goals is to keep the web open and free. He believes that everyone should be able to access information and share ideas without restrictions, and that the web should not be controlled by a small number of powerful organisations.

World Wide Web Consortium

To help guide the development of the web, he founded the World Wide Web Consortium, also known as W3C. This organisation creates standards that ensure websites work properly across different devices and browsers, helping to keep the web consistent and reliable.

Privacy and Data

In recent years, he has become increasingly concerned about how personal data is used online. He believes that individuals should have more control over their own information, rather than companies collecting and using it without clear permission.

New Ideas

To address these concerns, he has worked on new projects such as Solid, which aims to give users control over their own data. This system allows people to decide who can access their information and how it is used.

Challenges Today

The modern web faces several challenges, including misinformation, privacy issues, and the concentration of power in large companies. Tim Berners-Lee has spoken openly about these problems and continues to work on solutions to improve the situation.

Looking Ahead

Looking to the future, his goal is to create a web that is safer, more trustworthy, and more beneficial for everyone. He continues to advocate for a system where users are in control and where the web remains a force for good in society.
